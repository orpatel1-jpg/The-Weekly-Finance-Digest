Money Tips

Budget Your Spending

Weekly Tracking: Monitor your expenses for food, gas, and school supplies every seven days to catch overspending before it becomes a monthly problem.

Identify Patterns: Categorizing your costs helps you see exactly where "leakage" occurs, such as frequent takeout or subscription services you no longer use.

Set Limits: Use the data from your tracking to set realistic caps on non-essential categories, ensuring your "needs" are always covered first.

Start Saving Early

Every dollar saved in your 20s has significantly more growth potential than a dollar saved later, thanks to the power of time and reinvested earnings.

Consistently setting aside even small amounts creates a financial safety net that prevents you from relying on high-interest credit cards when unexpected costs arise.

Starting early allows you to break down large future milestones like travel, a first home, or a car into manageable monthly contributions rather than overwhelming lump sums.

Use Credit Carefully

Build Your Score: Use credit cards for small, recurring purchases to establish a strong credit history, which is essential for future apartment leases or car loans.

Pay in Full: Always pay your statement balance in full every month to avoid high-interest charges and late fees that can quickly spiral into debt.

Monitor Utilization: Keep your spending below 30% of your total credit limit to maintain a healthy credit score and demonstrate financial responsibility to lenders.
